Asian shares lifted by improving global data
02/02/2012
Financial Times (FT.Com)*FT.com
pq
Asian shares jumped after improving manufacturing data in China, US and Europe soothed concerns about the global economic outlook.
The FTSE Asia Pacific index gained 1.2 per cent to 241.09, its highest close in three months.
Japan's Nikkei 225 Stock Average rose 0.8 per cent as the Tokyo Stock Exchange was investigating the cause of the glitch that led to the suspension of trading in 241 companies.
US factory output grew at its strongest pace in seven months in January while Germany's manufacturing sector expanded for the first time in fourth months and China's manufacturing output was also better than expected.
The best gains came from China, as mainland stocks on the Shanghai Composite gained 2 per cent to 2,312.6 and Hong Kong's Hang Seng also climbed 2 per cent to 20,739.45.
Commodity stocks leapt on hopes of industry consolidation after it was revealed London-listed mining group Xstrata was in talks with trader Glencore about a merger. Hong Kong-listed Glencore shares jumped 5.5 per cent to HK$53.45.
Elsewhere in the region, Australian mining groups BHP Billiton and Rio Tinto jumped 1.9 per cent to A$37.62 and 2.9 per cent to A$70.72 respectively.
Lynas, the rare earth metals miner, soared 19.1 per cent to A$1.59 after winning a temporary licence to run a rare earth refinery in Malaysia.
Sydney's S&P/ASX 200 rose 1 per cent to 4,267.8.
Banking shares were higher across the board with Japan's Mitsubishi UFJ Financial Group up 2.5 per cent to Y368 after posting an 18 per cent jump in its nine-month net profit.
Nomura Holdings climbed 7.1 per cent to Y300 after reporting a surprise return to profit in its October-December quarter thanks to gains in European investment banking revenues.
Daiwa Securities rallied 4.5 per cent to Y281 and Sumitomo Mitsui Trust Holdings rose 3.3 per cent to Y254.
The improving global economic outlook boosted Japanese automakers with Toyota Motor up 1.8 per cent to Y2,906 after reporting a 7.5 per cent rise in January US sales and Honda Motor up 1.8 per cent to Y2,722 after posting a 9.3 per cent US sales gain.
Tokyo's Nikkei 225 Average climbed 0.8 per cent to 8,876.82.
Seoul's Kospi Composite climbed 1.3 per cent to 1,984.3, helped by a 7.4 per cent gain to Won90,500 for LG Electronics after the world's third-largest maker of mobile phones reported a narrower net loss for last year.
