Tokyo stocks open up 0.63%
02/01/2012
Agence France Presse*Agence France Presse -- English
fv
Tokyo stocks opened 0.63 percent higher on Thursday following solid gains in US and European markets overnight.
The Nikkei 225 index at the Tokyo Stock Exchange opened up 55.49 points at 8,865.28 despite the bourse experiencing a technical glitch.
But a sharp rally wasunlikely given sluggish Japanese corporate earnings results, said Kenichi Hirano, operating officer at Tachibana Securities.
"Japan stocks are lagging, but are not necessarily undervalued," he told Dow Jones Newswires.
The exchange suspended trading of 241 issues, including Sony, due to a technical glitch. It was not known when trade in the shares would resume.
Investors were watching how the market would react to an announcement by Sony after Wednesday's close that Howard Stringer is to step down as president and CEO of the troubled tech giant.
Kazuo Hirai, a games and music veteran, will replace the Welsh-born American.
The euro bought $1.3169and 100.32 yen in early Asian trade, compared with $1.3161 and 100.31 yen in New York late Wednesday. The dollar was at 76.17 yen against 76.21 yen in New York.
In New York on Wednesdaythe Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 0.66 percent to close at 12,716.46, while the tech-heavy Nasdaq added 1.22 percent to 2,848.27.
