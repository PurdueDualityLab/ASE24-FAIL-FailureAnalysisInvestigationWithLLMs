UPDATE1: Stocks up on global economic hopes, trading partially suspended by glitch
02/01/2012
Kyodo News
fv
TOKYO, Feb. 2 -- Tokyostocks ended higher Thursday on hopes for a global economic recovery sparked by upbeat economic data around the world, after morning trading was partially suspended at Japan's biggest stock exchange due to a system error.
The 225-issue NikkeiStock Average closed up 67.03 points, or 0.76 percent, from Wednesday at 8,876.82. The broader Topix index of all First Section issues on the Tokyo Stock Exchange finished up 4.49 points, or 0.59 percent, at 762.45.
Tokyo Stock ExchangeGroup Inc. suspended the trading of 241 issues or about 10 percent of all issues listed on the bourse, including major stocks such as Sony and Hitachi, during the morning session due to problems with the bourse's trading information distribution system.
The bourse resumed trading of the suspended shares when the afternoon session began at 12:30 p.m.
Gainers were led by thesecurities, sea transport and insurance sectors. Among decliners were the electricity and gas, oil and coal products, and electrical machinery sectors.
Sentiment was lifted inTokyo on improved manufacturing data in the eurozone and China, while U.S. manufacturing and jobs-related figures were also regarded as good enough, despite falling short of analysts' expectations, brokers said. All the indicators reported were for January.
''Concerns about apossible slowdown in the U.S. economy abated on a series of promising macroeconomic data, raising hopes for U.S. jobs data due out Friday,'' said Tatsunori Kawai, chief strategist at kabu.com Securities Co.
Stocks were alsosupported, despite profit outlook cuts by many Japanese companies for the current business year, as investors have generally factored in the projections amid poor market conditions due to flooding in Thailand and the persistent appreciation of the yen, brokers said.
''Investors are morelikely to react to changes in dividends or to announcements of stock repurchases by companies, which are moves that cannot be foreseen,'' said Masayoshi Okamoto, equity strategist at Jujiya Securities Co.
Automakers gained after aU.S. research firm said new car sales in the country in January rose 11.4 percent for the eighth straight month of year-on-year increase on the back of the economic recovery.
Toyota Motor rose 51 yen, or 1.8 percent, to 2,906 yen and Honda Motor climbed 48 yen, or 1.8 percent, to 2,722 yen.
Nomura Holdingssurged 20 yen, or 7.1 percent, to 300 yen after its net profit in the October-to-December period rose 33 percent from a year before, exceeding market forecasts.
Sharp plunged by a dailyallowable limit of 100 yen, or 15.9 percent, to end at a 31-year-low of 528 yen after it said the previous day it is likely to post its largest-ever net loss of 290 billion yen for the fiscal year ending March 31. It also said it plans to pay a full-year dividend of 10 yen per share, down from the previous year's 17 yen.
On the First Section,advancing stocks outnumbered declining ones 933 to 591, with 146 others unchanged. Trading volume on the main section rose to 2,251.87 million shares from Wednesday's 2,150.14 million.
