Tokyo stocks open higher on hopes for global economic recovery
02/01/2012
Kyodo News*Japan Economic Newswire
fv
TOKYO, Feb. 2 -- Tokyostocks opened higher Thursday on hopes for a global economic recovery following improvements in manufacturing data in the eurozone and China for January in trading hit by a system glitch.
The Tokyo Stock ExchangeGroup Inc. said before the opening bell that trading in 241 issues has been suspended due to problems in the bourse's trading information distribution system.
In the first 15 minutesof trading, the 225-issue Nikkei Stock Average rose 71.75 points, or 0.81 percent, from Wednesday to 8,881.54. The broader Topix index of all First Section issues on the Tokyo Stock Exchange was up 7.05 points, or 0.93 percent, to 765.01.
Sentiment was lifted alsoas U.S. manufacturing and jobs-related figures released overnight were construed as good enough, despite falling short of analyst expectations, brokers said.
