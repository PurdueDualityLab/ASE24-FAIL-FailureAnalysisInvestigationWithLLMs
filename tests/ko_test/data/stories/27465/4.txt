Anti-terrorism drill suffers glitches
11/17/2003
The Guardian (Charlottetown, Prince Edward Island)
ln
OTTAWA (CP) -- The biggest counter-terrorism exercise since the terrorist attacks of Sept. 11, 2001, was plagued by poor information sharing, the absence of key Canadian departments and bothersome technical glitches, internal government memos reveal.
A major problem exposed by Exercise Topoff 2 was a lack of cross-border communication between some Canadian and U.S. officials due to the recent shift of many American personnel into the new Department of Homeland Security.s
