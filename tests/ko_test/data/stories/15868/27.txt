AIR TRAFFIC BACK TO NORMAL IN THE WEST AFTER A RADAR GLITCH GROUNDS HUNDREDS OF FLIGHTS
10/20/2000
CNBC News Transcripts
ln
LIZ CLAMAN, anchor:
Air traffic out West is back to normal this morning after a radar glitch grounded hundreds of flights. Flights going into and out of parts of California, Nevada and Utah were canceled after a radar system controlling the entire region broke down. The system has now been restored.
